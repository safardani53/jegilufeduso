Steve Harvey slams his cue card and forces Family Feud contestant to apologize over naughty answer

FAMILY Feud host Steve Harvey has slammed his cue cards on the desk.

A contestant on the game show was forced to apologize for their NSFW answer.

On a recent episode of Family Feud, two contestants came up to the podium to answer a question.

Steve, 66, asked them: "Name something Mrs. Claus wants for Christmas that can't be made by the elves."

The contestant on the left, Jessica, buzzed in and said: "Santa Claus uh-heh."

Jessica didn't want to say the actual word for what she was thinking so she just said: "The stuff. The stuff."

Steve gave her wide eyes, slammed down his cue cards and said: "What were you thinking saying that with Miss Diane up here?"

Diane was an elderly contestant who went up against Jessica.

"Do you see what she's saying with Miss Diane standing right here? I'm not saying this," Steve told the producers.

'I'M SORRY!'

Jessica apologized saying: "I'm sorry. I'm sorry" over and over again.

When she turned her apology to Diane, she said that she forgave Jessica.

Steve explained that Jessica was a little young so that's why she gave that naughty answer.

He then turned toward the screen to see if Jessica's answer was up there.

"Santa's South Pole" was on the board and was the number one answer.

After Jessica said she would play and went back to her spot at the desk, Steve walked up to her.

"Don't look at me!" she told the host.

"What's wrong with you? In front of Miss Diane," Steve said.

"I know your momma raised your better than that."

G-HOST-ED

This isn't the first time Steve has been dramatic with his cue cards.

In November, fans thought the host almost called it quits over a contestant's answer.

"What’s something people run across their lips?" he asked the contestants.

Contestant Jim confidently guessed a more literal interpretation, and said: "Gravy."

Steve sat down on the podium and laughed because he was sure that answer would not be on the board.

"You're just trying to give the safest answer you can. You know good and d**n well that ain't up there," he said.

However, gravy was put under the category "Food/juicy cherry," which received two points from the survey.
