Two teens seriously injured and third wounded after broad daylight stabbing

TWO teenagers have been seriously injured and a third was wounded after a broad daylight triple stabbing.

Police were scrambled to reports of a stabbing and discovered a 17-year-old suffering from serious and potentially life-threatening injuries.

A 16-year-old boy was also seriously wounded and was rushed to hospital, while a third boy, also aged 16, is understood to have suffered minor injuries.

Police confirmed a 16-year-old boy was arrested on suspicion of three counts of grievous bodily harm with intent and remains in custody.

Witnesses spoke of their "terror" today following the frenzy in Littlehampton, West Sussex at around 2.45pm.

Claire Horn, who was with her two-year-old daughter at the time, said: "The paramedics and police cornered off the area and put Superdrug shopping baskets over any evidence.

"As we entered Divine Nail Bar, people were going to shops to hide.

"Once inside Divine Nails there was lots of police and paramedics and lots of people hovering around – it was pretty scary.

“I think everyone in the area were shocked and panicked. I was worried to come out of the shop as I had my two-year-old daughter.

"We could see blood around the Superdrug shop window area."

Another local added: "There was blood all over the floor. It was terrifying.

"There were two boys on the floor getting first aid. One of them looked in a really bad condition."

Police have cordoned off the busy high street while they hunt for clues.

A spokesman said: "There are two suspects currently outstanding and police believe the five are known to each other.

"Investigation is ongoing."
