As for education, Gheorghe attended Cluj University. He completed his collegiate career there and became the national champion in 1992.

Gheorghe Muresan | Basketball Career

After graduating from college, Muresan debuted professionally in the French League for the team Pau-Orthez in 1992 for a year.

He soon became a fan favorite and also managed to take the French League Cup.

It was later year in 1993, that Gheorghe got selected by Washington Bullets in the 1993 NBA draft. He prolonged his career for seven long years in NBA until injuries wrecked him.

To talk about his best season, Gheorghe went out terrifically good for the season 1995, where he notched 14.5 points, 9.6 rebounds, and 2.26 blocks per game.

He was also titled the NBA’s Most Improved Player after leading the league-leading with 58.4 percent of his field goals.

For the next season, too, Gheorghe led his team in field goal percentage with a 60.4 percent average.

He signed with the New Jersey Jets and appeared for the final 31 games of his career.

And this was a wrap to his NBA career, which he accomplished with 9.8 points, 6.4 rebounds, 0.5 assists, and 1.48 blocks per game.

Well, Gheorghe had another bout with the Pau-Orthez where he led his team to grab the French League title in 2001.
