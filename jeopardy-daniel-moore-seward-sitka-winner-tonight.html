Jeopardy! contestant mocked for far-fetched final answer as promising champ Daniel Moore struggle

A JEOPARDY! contestant was criticized for an incorrect Final Jeopardy guess that didn't quite hold water on Monday's episode.

The off-kilter answer came as exciting champion Daniel Moore prevailed with a less sure-fire second win.

Daniel, a contract compliance analyst from Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, faced Leann Craumer from Maryland and Jerry Powers from New Jersey.

He returned with $25,000 after notching a textbook Jeopardy! debut on Friday.

Host Ken Jennings, 49, even opened tonight's show by coining the debut: “Daniel domination.”

The cool, calm, and cash-collecting contestant had nabbed a stunning 30 correct clues and all three Daily Doubles- but things went differently this time.

Daniel missed the opening Daily Double in the Jeopardy! round drawing a complete blank.

While he got in 21 clues correct in Double Jeopardy!, he also got eight clues incorrect.

Going into Final Jeopardy, Daniel wound up with $9,800, Leanne had $7,400, and Jerry had $4,200.

​​Final Jeopardy! under "Government Officials" read: “In 1867 he wrote to General Rousseau, 'On arriving at Sitka…you will receive from the Russian commissioner the formal transfer.'"

Most read in Entertainment

Daniel and Jerry were correct with [Secrety of State William H.] “Seward,” as the clue referred to Sitka, Alaska.

Alaska was bought by the United States in 1867 from Russia for $7.2M. The Treaty with Russia was negotiated and signed by Seward.

However, Leanne was incorrect and guessed “Napoleon” dropping $602 - she had a slightly embarrassed smirk while revealing it.

Daniel added $5,000 to win with $14,800 for a two-day total of $39,800.

However, since Jerry was also correct he was in the lead by $1 before Daniel revealed his response- which he needed to get right to win.

FANS REACT: 'NAPOLEON?'

Fans were slightly surprised by the Napoleon guess and didn't hold back on social media.

One person wrote under a YouTube clip: “Napoleon in 1867 Sitka?”

A second tweeted: "Napoleon? #Jeopardy"

“Final was sooooo easy,” another person wrote, though another defended they had “no clue.”

A fourth wrote: "I didn't get it either but if one knew the year the U.S. got Alaska it would be easy to guess from that."

A fifth explained: “Aha! Gotta be 'Sewards Folly!' So of course Secretary of State Seward, who suffered ridicule after the purchase.”

Others were happy for Daniel, but after such a strong debut, some were concerned he may be losing steam.

One Reddit user wrote: “I was worried we were about to see another game two collapse after an impressive debut. Glad he was able to recover and notch another win--he clearly has a strong knowledge base.

Another agreed: "That was a tough game for him. I hope he's able to return to his form from Friday's game tomorrow."

And an eigth wrote: “Hoping that Daniel will keep going and going. It would be nice to have a multiple-game winner to end the season."

Daniel is a lifelong watcher of Jeopardy! and revealed in a bonus video that his mom is from Port-au-Prince, Haiti.

Fans are loving his smarts nonetheless, as one more wrote to Twitter: "Daniel is really impressive. I hope this is the first of many wins and he sticks around a while."
