Rapper Jim Jones reveals he french kissed his own MOM when she taught him how to smooch

RAPPER Jim Jones shocked fans during a podcast appearance when he revealed that his mother kissed him on the mouth when he was a teenager to demonstrate a good kissing technique.

Jones is a member of Dipset, a Harlem rap collective, and he made the startling confession in an episode of Angela Yee’s Lip Service podcast that dropped on Thursday.

"My mom taught me how to kiss when I was younger," Jones disclosed, sparking confusion from Yee.

She asked what instructions "Mama" Jones gave him.

"What did she tell you to do?" Yee asked.

But Jones clarified: "She showed me with her mouth," he said.

"She kissed you?" Yee exclaimed.

Jones responded, "It's my mama!"

Even though Yee seemed shocked, she accepted that Jones had a different family dynamic than she did.

"My parents barely kissed me on the cheek," Yee admitted.

Jones pointed out that his mother, Nancy Jones, had him when she was only 17 and said young parents often have a bond closer to that of a sibling relationship with their children.

"Look how they act with their babies," he said of young parents.

"It's like they have a little sister or a little brother."

Commenters on YouTube and Instagram, where The Shade Room reposted the controversial clip, were divided in response to the startling revelation.

Some thought that it was Jim's family business and that if he didn't see it as a problem, then fans shouldn't be upset.

"Who are we to assume this bothers him," asked an Instagram commenter.

"Now if he would of said 'yeah it bothered me,' that’s different."

"Jim is as real as it comes and very smart," said one YouTube commenter.

Others found the exchange horrifying and said the revelation explained the dynamic between Jones, his mother, and his fiancée Chrissy Lampkin.

"He was too confident in that response like that was normal. NO SIR!!!" said one fan on Instagram.

"Imagine if a Father taught his Daughter to tongue kiss?" argued a YouTube commenter.

"This is disgusting and explains their entire dynamic on the show," referring to Nancy's appearances on Love & Hip Hop and its spin-off Chrissy & Mr. Jones.
