Ish Wainright: Family, Salary &amp; Kids [2023 Update]

Ishmail Carzell Wainright, better known as Ish Wainright, is an American-Ugandan basketball player who is currently signed on a two-way contract with the Phoenix Suns of the NBA.

Ish’s grandfather was a professional basketball player who played for the Boston Celtics in the 1959-60 season.

Ish has officially reached his total number of games on the Suns’ roster as a two-way player. What does this actually mean? Is he becoming a free agent?

He still has a chance at rejoining the team if they were to convert his contract into a standard one. The team does not have a G League affiliate currently to send him for development, though.

With the arrival of Kevin Durant to the already strong team just before the end of the 2023 NBA trade deadline, the Suns stand fourth in Western Conference with 32 wins and 27 losses as of this writing.

Early Life & Education

Ishmail Carzell Wainright was born on September 12, 1994, in Kansas City, Missouri, United States, to his father, Calvin Wainright, and mother, Mary Collins.

On October 31, 2022, Wainright lost hi father Calvin, who was a pastor and youth basketball coach in Kansas City, Missouri. For his work in the city, he received the Kansas City People’s Choice Awards Humanitarian Award in 2020.

As mentioned before, his grandfather Maurice King played in the NBA, which Ish learned from his mother’s side of the family while playing for Baylor.

Ish also has a younger brother named Amaad, who, just like him, plays basketball. Amaad played collegiate basketball at Kansas State University in the NCAA.

Coming about the education side of things, Ish played two seasons at Montrose Christian School under the head coach Stu Vetter and began his prep career at Raytown South High School.

A consensus top-75 recruit, ESPN ranked Ish No. 28 in the 2013 class, and Rivals.com ranked him No. 52.

Coming out of high school, he committed to play for the Baylor Bears at Baylor University.

He played four season of basketball with the Bears and also tried his hands in football as a tight end for a season where he had four catches for 34 yards and 2 TDs.

As a matter of fact, his football passion took him as far as joining the Buffalo Bills of the NFL as an undrafted free agent. However, after failing to make it to the final roster, he left football and focused solely on basketball.

Career

Wainright went undrafted in the 2017 NBA Draft and signed with German basketball team Nürnberg Falcons BC on September 3, 2018.

After a season, he signed with another German team Rasta Vechta on July 2, 2019, where he averaged 10.5 points per game.

Likewise, in 2020 he signed with SIG Strasbourg of France.

After playing in German and French basketball teams, on August 8, 2021, Ish’s NBA dream came true when he signed a two-year non-guaranteed deal with the Raptors.

Nevertheless, the Raptors waived him on October 16.

He signed a two-way deal with the Suns just six days later and made his debut on November 19 in a win against the Mavericks.

On April 6, 2022, nearing the end of the 2021-22 season, Wainright scored his career-high 20 points against LA Clippers.

On August 4, 2022, Wainright signed another two-way contract with the Suns for the 2022-23 season.

And by the mid-season, the player already reached his total number of games on the Suns’ roster as a two-way player.

So, is he becoming a free agent?

He still has a chance to rejoin the team if the Suns were to convert his contract into a standard one. They do not have a G League affiliate to send him to, though.

Ish Wainright Has Dual Nationality

Born in Kansas City, Missouri, Ish is an American by birth. But Mike Schmitz and George Galanopolous, Uganda men’s national basketball team coaches, pursued him to become a become a naturalized Ugandan citizen.

They also convinced him to secure an African passport to help build the team.

And this is how the American national became Ugandan-American. Ish represents Uganda’s national basketball team.
