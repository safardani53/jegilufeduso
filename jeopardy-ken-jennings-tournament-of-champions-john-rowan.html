Jeopardy! fans go wild as 'unexpected' champ scores 'total blowout' in twist Tournament of Champions

JEOPARDY! champ John Focht put his game face on for Wednesday's episode with a "total blowout" victory that fans were not expecting.

He scored the Tournament of Champions' first runaway game.

The software team leader from Texas faced Rowan Ward - a writer and Second Chance competition winner from Chicago - and Courtney Shah - a community college instructor from Oregon.

Ken, 48, is now looming over the "stacked" Tournament of Champions which started Monday and is airing instead of regular episodes for the next three weeks.

Many fans were expecting Rowan, who dominated the never-before-done comeback contest just days ago nabbing one of the last two slots of 21 - to keep soaring.

However, the calm and collected John quickly emerged as the one to watch instead.

Focht tore into the game in the Double Jeopardy round, wagering $8K when he landed on a randomly placed "Daily Double" clue.

"Wow, alright!" host Ken said, surprised by the big bet.

"To resolve a spat with Greece, in 2019 it added a direction to its name," it read.

John correctly responded "What is: North Macedonia" without hestitation, and one clue later, hit another and added $3K more.

By the last round, John towered above his rivals with $32,000, Rowan had an impressive $10,800, and Courtney had $5,200.

Final Jeopardy read: "This place from a 1933 novel lies in the Valley of Blue Moon, below a peak called Karakal"

No one was correct with "What is Shangri-La?" but it didn't matter - going in, John had a runaway game.

His score was so high that barring a massive bet he'd already have won going into the last round.

"John put on a clinic," Ken had to admit on stage.

'HOLY HELL!'

The champ knew as much and smiled a huge grin, wagering $0, he will advance with $32K, the biggest win of the tournament yet.

Fans went wild, especially he won 4 games in Season 37 (the minimum to qualify for the winners' tournament).

And he trounced rising star Rowan, the blue-haired barnburner that many people thought couldn't be stopped.

One viewer wrote on Reddit: "Damnnn. a total blowout… and not for the person expected."

Another agreed: "I honestly thought Rowan was going to take the advantage, but holy hell, John just completely swept the floor in this game. Many congratulations to him."

A third wrote: "John absolutely destroyed. Even without the Daily Doubles, he was well and clear of the other two contestants.

"Of course, I wish rowan had found at least one [Daily Double] so the game was more interesting," they added.

Another similarly lamented: "Shame we won’t the get Rowan vs. Matt Amodio rematch, but John thoroughly earned the win."

John earlier revealed on Twitter that his mother and grandmother got into a car accident coming to watch him play - but made it uninjured - so it was all the more meaningful.

John, Tyler Rhode, Andrew He, and Monday's victor Maureen O'Neill will now advance to the semi-finals of the fierce $250K faceoff.

LEAGUE OF LEGENDS

This year's Tournament of Champions started airing on October 31.

It features more super-champions (or contestants who have won more than 10 games) than any year prior.

Returning champs like Mattea Roach (23 wins and $560K), Amy Schneider (40 wins and $1.3M), Matt Amodio (38 wins and $1.5M) have three of the five of the longest winning streaks in history and will play next week.

They are automatically being put into the semi-finals since they're such big winners.

After the three-game semi-finals is a first-to-three final which could last up to seven episodes.

Fans have been itching for a big win like this since the beloved Ryan Long lost on night one and so far the games had low scores and morale.

"Been an ok TOC so far. I was surprised that some didn’t do better especially first game," one fan earlier surmised on Instagram.

Regular episodes and reigning ace Cris Pannullo will return mid-November when it all concludes.
