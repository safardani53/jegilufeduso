About Austin Reaves

Professional basketball player Austin Reaves was born on May 29, 1998, in Newark, Arkansas, to Nicole Wilkett and Brian Reaves, both of whom played basketball at Arkansas State.

Austin grew up with his brother Spencer Reaves who played college basketball for North Greenville and Central Missouri.

Austin went to Cedar Ridge High School, where he won back-to-back Class 2A state titles in his first two years.

After high school, he played college basketball for the Wichita State Shockers and the Oklahoma Sooners.

Reaves declared for the 2021 NBA Draft, forgoing remaining college eligibility but went undrafted and signed a two-way contract with the Los Angeles Lakers on August 3, 2021.

On September 27, his contract was converted to the standard one. Austin made his NBA debut against the Suns on October 22 in a 115-105 loss.

He scored a career-high 35 points on March 19, 2023, in a 111-105 win against the Magic, in which he scored the final 10 points for the Lakers.

“Too Small”

They met at United Center on March 30, and as the Lakers were capping off their bounce-back victory, Austin Reaves scored over Beverly and returned the message to the former Laker, dropping the “too small” gesture.
