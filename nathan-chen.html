Nathan Chen | Early Career

As he started working on skating seriously, Nathan first featured in the U.S. Junior Nationals for two years, in 2007 and 2008.

He first stood in tenth place, followed by the improvised third place in the Junior level.

Alongside that, for the same event, he bagged the silver medal in 2009, as he entered the intermediate level.

The following year, Chen had his first stepping stone; he became the youngest novice champion in U.S. Figure Skating history.

Next year, he partook in the same event, the 2011 U.S. Championships in Greensboro, North Carolina, where he saved his title for himself.

Additionally, jump specialists Rafael Arutyunyan and Genia Chernyshova prepared him further for the 2012 U.S. Championships on January 24.

Junior International Level

Nathan Chen’s major international debut came in September 2012 through JGP Austria in Linz.

He had a successful career opening with a gold medal in the field as he recorded 222 points.

Unfortunately, due to his leg injury, he couldn’t continue further; hence, he had to partake the following year.

Thus, in his junior level, he maintained his bronze medal at the 2013 U.S. Championships in Omaha, Nebraska.

Chen had a good winning streak as he claimed gold for the ISU Junior Grand Prix in Mexico, the Junior Grand Prix Final in Japan, and bronze in the 2014 World Junior Championships in Bulgaria.

Furthermore, he also broke the six-year-old record of junior men.

Senior National Level

In late 2014, Chen moved into the senior level of skating as he made his senior debut at the Pacific Sectional Championships.

In 2015, Chen had his heel injury, so; he was only in the scaled-back versions of his short and long programs.

Later, in 2016, Chen bagged the gold medal in the ISU Junior Grand Prix series.

Besides, in the 2016 U.S. Championship, he also became the first American to land two quadruple jumps in the short program, followed by the first to have four quadruples in the long program.

Chen couldn’t attend the 2016 World Junior Championships due to his left hip surgery.

Thus, this took him a month to heal under the U.S. Olympic Training Center (OTC) rehabilitation center.

Senior Internation Level

During Chen’s senior level, he was guided by the coaches Marina Zueva and Oleg Epstein.

Altogether, Chen had an overwhelming start to his senior international level with a gold medal in the 2016 C.S. Finlandia Trophy.

The same year, he also broke the Olympic champion Evan Lysacek’s record of 90.3 with his own 93.85.

Chen was also the second-youngest male skater to earn a medal at GPF.

Altogether, Chen had gained the 2017 Four Continents as he won the short program, followed by being the second in the 2017 World Team Trophy.

Nathan Chen | 2022 Winter Olympics Beijing

Entering the Olympics season, Nathan was choreographed by Shae-Lynn Bourne, and under her, he managed to land on five different quads in a competition.

During his tenure, Chen starred alongside his U.S. Team members, Jeremy Abbott, Karen Chen, and Mirai Nagasu.

The 22-year-old Chen skated to Elton John and Bernie Taupin’s “Rocket Man” for an almost flawless free skate to achieve an Olympic record total score of 332.61, improving on a disappointing fifth-place finish from four years earlier.

“It had always been a dream of mine, and it’s a pretty daunting mountain,”

Chen won the gold medal with five quad jumps while skating to the soundtrack of “Rocketman.”
