Woman claims she is missing 7million worth of gold after Hatton Garden robbery in 2015

A WOMAN has claimed she had £7million worth of gold stolen from her deposit box by the Hatton Garden raid gang.

She has come forward after realising the fortune has been missing since the heist in 2015.

Detectives are now investigating the claim, which would take the total amount stolen in the break-in to around £21million.

Last year six men were convicted over the break-in at Hatton Garden Safe Deposit.

The gang – nicknamed “Dad’s Army” due to their old age – were handed sentences of six and seven years.

The gang ransacked 73 safe deposit boxes after used a drill to bore their way into the vault wall, before stealing gold, diamonds and sapphires.

They managed to escape with £14million worth of goods during the planned heist over the Easter break.

Ringleaders John "Kenny" Collins, 75; Daniel Jones, 61; Terry Perkins, 67; and the eldest of the group, Brian Reader, 77, pleaded guilty to conspiracy to commit burglary last September.

Collins, Jones and Perkins were each given a seven-year prison term, while Reader was jailed for six years and three months.

William Lincoln, 60, and Carl Wood, 59, were found guilty of the same offence and one count of and conspiracy to conceal, convert or transfer criminal property.

Lincoln was handed a seven-year sentence, while Wood was jailed for six years.

Hugh Doyle was jailed for 21 months, suspended for two years, after being found guilty of concealing, converting or transferring criminal property between January 1 and May 19 last year.
