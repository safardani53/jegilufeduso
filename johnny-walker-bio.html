Johnny Walker: Career

After coming through the amateur ranks in Brazil, Walker made his professional MMA debut at Circuito Invictus de MMA 2 on December 14, 2013.

Having entered the fight with a reputation for knocking out opponents easily, Jhonny was a considerable favorite to win the contest.

Subsequently, staying true to all the expectations, the Brazilian fighter won the bout via TKO in the 49th second of the first round. Hence, Walker announced himself into the professional MMA scene with a bang.

Thereafter, the 26-year-old fought with Vitor Casanova and Joao Silva, with the outcome being the same as his debut match, first-round TKOs. Consequently, a lot of hype was growing up for the talented striker.

However, Walker was brought back down to Earth in his fourth professional fight as he lost to Wagner Prado. To explain, Prado TKO’d the Rio de Janeiro native in the 3-minute 40-second mark of the second round to seal an upset.

Consequently, Walker earned the first title fight of his professional career against Klidson Abreu for the vacant Samurai FC Heavyweight Championship. But, sadly, the Rio de Janeiro native lost the fight due to a rear-naked choke.

Consequently, in his next two fights, Walker seemed to lack a bit of confidence as he lost one and won the other.

Nevertheless, the talented 6 feet 6 striker dusted himself and went on a nine-fight win streak from March 25, 2017, to November 2, 2019. Likewise, in those nine fights, Jhonny had some memorable moments that eventually saw him land a prestigious UFC contract.
