Javonte Green: Kids, Wife &amp; Net Worth [2023 Update]

Javonte Green, an American-Montenegrin NBA player, plays as a small forward/shooting guard for the Chicago Bulls. Before his NBA debut in 2019, the player was already a big name in the European basketball leagues.

Green, an undrafted agent of the 2015 NBA Draft, toured with basketball teams: Peixegalego (of Spain), Trieste (Italy) & Ratiopharm (Germany) before stepping into NBA.

On a two-year contract with Chicago Bulls signed in 2021, the jersey number #24 player is committed through the 2022-23 season.

While off from the NBA courts, Javonte Green loves to spend time with his children.

No doubt, basketball career success has blessed him with perks but also walked him through injuries multiple times. In 2020, he had surgery to repair a meniscal tear after an injury in his right knee.

With only 28 games in the 2022-23 season, Javonte was sidelined after late December 2022 to recover from his knee injury.

Javonte Green’s Parents & Siblings

Green was born in Petersburg, Virginia, on the 23rd of July, 1993. He is the youngest child of his parents: Jeffrey Mason and Maxine Green; he has three siblings, Kara Green, Kiara Green, and Deviante Green.

As reported, Javonte’s parents separated when he was young, so his mother, who worked in a factory, raised him as a single parent.

Due to high crime rates in Petersburg, his mom wanted him to be away from those. So when the future NBA star asked to live with his cousins, Maxine agreed right away.

Once Green turned 12 years old, he moved to Alberta, Virginia, to live with his aunt Kim Mason, a special education teacher at the local elementary school.

Further information on his family background remains under wraps.

High School & Collegiate Basketball Career

The Petersburg-born attended Brunswick High School in Lawrenceville under Bryant Stith, a veteran NBA player-turned-coach. He led the team to VHSL Group AA Division 3 state title.

A three-sport athlete at high school, Green chose to pursue a career with the hoops during his senior year.

For his college basketball, Javonte Green enrolled at Radford University (the only NCAA Division I to offer him a basketball scholarship).

With the Radford Highlanders, he finished his freshmen year with 10.2 points per game which jumped to 14.6 points per game as a sophomore.

In his junior year, he set the school single-season steals record with 68, and he was awarded First Team All-Big South after averaging 16.9 points, 8.1 rebounds, and 1.9 steals per game.

Green completed his collegiate career as Radford’s all-time leader in rebounds (1,064), steals (245), games played (133), and scoring with 1,911 points.

Undrafted In The 2015 NBA Draft

Despite being a promising figure of Radford, Javonte Green was undrafted in the 2015 NBA Draft.

So, he would spend his three years with European basketball teams (in Spain, Italy & German) until his NBA entry in 2019.

His first-ever professional signing was with the Spanish third-division team Marin Peixegalego on September 11, 2015. There he averaged 6.7 rebounds, 2.2 steals, and 18 points per game.

After that, on July 11, 2016, Green signed with Pallacanestro Trieste of the Serie A2 Basket in Italy. In 45 games with Trieste in the 2016–17 season, he averaged 15.6 points, 5.5 rebounds, and 2.4 steals per game.

The Basketball Bundesliga team Ratiopharm Ulm acquired Javonte on July 25, 2018. In 51 games between the league and EuroCup, Green averaged 13.8 points, 4.7 rebounds, 1.6 assists, and 2.3 steals per game.

NBA Debut & Journey

In the 2019 NBA Summer League, Javonte Green played for the Boston Celtics in five games & averaged 10.8 points, 4.8 rebounds, 2.8 assists, and 1.8 steals per game.

This impressive gameplay saw him an entry into the NBA after the Celtics signed him on a two-year contract on July 8, 2019.

On October 25, 2019, Green made his NBA debut as a defensive replacement in the last five seconds of the first half in a 112-106 win against the Toronto Raptors.

Then, on November 7 that year, he scored 12 points (his first-ever NBA points) in a 108-87 victory over the Hornets.

It followed his first-ever start with the Celtics against Atlanta Hawks on February 3, 2020, where he played for 18 minutes with 3 rebounds, 1 assist, and no points.

After two seasons with Boston Celtics, Javonte Green was traded to Chicago Bulls as a part of three-team trade (between the Celtics, Bulls & Washington).

With the Bulls, he wore jersey number #24, which was once worn by some iconic players like Bill Cartwright, Tyrus Thomas, and Lauri Markkanen.
